Section 1: Explanation
What is Prompt Engineering?
Prompt Engineering is the skill of crafting clear, specific, and well-structured instructions for AI tools like Claude. Think of it like giving directions — the clearer and more detailed your directions, the more accurately someone arrives at the destination. With AI, your prompt is your direction.
Why does it matter?
AI tools respond to exactly what you write. A vague question gets a vague answer. A well-crafted prompt gives the AI the context, role, format, and goal it needs to deliver something genuinely useful. As AI becomes part of everyday work, Prompt Engineering is becoming a core professional skill — not just for developers, but for writers, marketers, educators, and business professionals.

Section 2: Weak vs Improved Prompt
Weak Prompt:

"Write about marketing."

Why it fails: No audience, no format, no goal, no length. The AI has to guess everything — and usually guesses wrong.
Engineered Prompt:

"Write 5 LinkedIn marketing tips for a SaaS startup targeting HR managers. Use bullet points, keep each tip under 50 words, and make them actionable."

Why it works: It defines the format (5 tips, bullet points), the audience (HR managers), the platform (LinkedIn), the length (50 words), and the tone (actionable).
